Figure 7 — Annual Job Posting Index

Unique Tech Job Postings by Year

Northern Ireland, Jan 2016 – Dec 2025 · Source: Lightcast / Perspective Economics

Job postings peaked at 4,565 in 2022, mirroring the post-Covid hiring surge. By 2025 (projected full year), postings had fallen to approximately 2,213 — a 51% decline from the 2022 high. This mirrors the NI Cyber Security Snapshot's finding of a 48% reduction in cyber postings since 2023, and reflects a global tech hiring correction rather than NI-specific weakness.

Figure 10 — Wage Distribution

Advertised Salary Breakdown

7,114 observations (22% of postings include salary) · Median: £30,016

Salary bands (£k)

The median NI tech salary of £30,016 sits significantly below the Cyber Snapshot's cyber-specific median of £53,300 — a £23,284 premium for specialist cyber roles. 18% of postings advertise £45,000+, pointing to a bifurcated market where senior/specialist tech commands significantly higher pay.

Figure 11 — Skills Composition

Top Specialised Skills: Demand vs Supply

% of postings requiring skill vs % of worker profiles listing it

In postings In profiles
Compositional mismatch: The data does not point to blanket skills undersupply. Cloud (AWS: +2.2pp) and Automation (+1.9pp) face genuine shortfalls, while Software Engineering (-6.6pp) and Project Management (-6.1pp) are oversupplied in profiles relative to postings — suggesting curriculum misalignment rather than a simple quantity problem.
Figure 12 — Entry-Level Access

Experience Requirements

Minimum years required per posting · Relevant to graduate absorption

Over 57% of postings list no experience requirement, suggesting considerable entry-level opportunity — however this is partially explained by undisclosed requirements. Postings explicitly requiring 0–1 years account for just 9.4%, echoing the Cyber Snapshot's finding that entry-level postings fell from 13% (2021) to 7% (2024) within cyber specifically.
